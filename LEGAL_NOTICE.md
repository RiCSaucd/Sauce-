The Vehicle Buyer Finder application accesses and processes personal information that may be protected under various federal and state laws. **All users must ensure full compliance with applicable laws before using this application.**

## Driver's Privacy Protection Act (DPPA)

### What is the DPPA?

The Driver's Privacy Protection Act (18 U.S.C. ยง 2721-2725) is a federal law that governs the disclosure of personal information from state motor vehicle records.

### Protected Information

The DPPA protects:
- Name, address, telephone number
- Social Security Number
- Driver identification number
- Photograph or image
- Medical or disability information
- Personal information on license plates

### Permissible Uses

DMV data may ONLY be accessed for these permissible uses:

1. **Use by government agencies** in carrying out official functions
2. **Motor vehicle safety and theft** activities
3. **Motor vehicle or driver safety recalls**
4. **Use in normal course of business** by legitimate businesses
5. **Use in research activities** (with proper authorization)
6. **Use in providing notice** to owners of vehicles
7. **Use by insurers** in connection with claims investigation
8. **Use in legal proceedings**
9. **Use by employers or prospective employers**
10. **Use by private investigators** (licensed and authorized)
11. **Use for bulk distribution** for surveys, marketing, or solicitation (WITH PRIOR CONSENT)

### Important Requirements

- **Written Authorization**: Must have documented authorization
- **Limited Purpose**: Can only use data for authorized purpose
- **No Resale**: Generally prohibited from reselling data
- **Consent Required**: Marketing uses require prior written consent from individuals

## Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA)

### Overview

The TCPA restricts telephone solicitations and the use of automated telephone equipment.

### Key Requirements

1. **Prior Express Written Consent**: Required before making marketing calls/texts
2. **Do Not Call Registry**: Must check and honor the National Do Not Call Registry
3. **Time Restrictions**: Cannot call before 8 AM or after 9 PM (recipient's local time)
4. **Identification**: Must identify yourself and provide callback number
5. **Opt-Out Mechanism**: Must honor opt-out requests immediately

### Penalties

- Up to $500 per violation
- Up to $1,500 per willful violation
- Private right of action for consumers

## CAN-SPAM Act

### Requirements for Email Marketing

If using collected email addresses for marketing:

1. **Don't use false or misleading header information**
2. **Don't use deceptive subject lines**
3. **Identify the message as an ad**
4. **Tell recipients where you're located**
5. **Tell recipients how to opt out**
6. **Honor opt-out requests promptly**
7. **Monitor what others are doing on your behalf**

### Penalties

- Up to $51,744 per violation
- Criminal penalties for aggravated violations

## Obtaining Authorization

### DMV API Access

**To obtain authorized DMV API access:**

1. **Contact State DMV**
   - Florida DMV: https://www.flhsmv.gov/
   - Phone: (850) 617-2000

2. **Required Documentation**
   - Business license
   - Purpose of data use
   - Data security plan
   - Proof of permissible use

3. **Data Use Agreement**
   - Sign DUA with DMV
   - Specify authorized use cases
   - Agree to audit requirements
   - Commit to data security

4. **API Credentials**
   - Receive authorized API key
   - Get endpoint documentation
   - Obtain user ID
   - Understand rate limits and restrictions

### Yellow Pages / Public Data

While Yellow Pages data is generally public:
- Review Yellow Pages Terms of Service
- Implement respectful scraping (rate limiting)
- Do not bypass technical restrictions
- Consider using official APIs if available

## Penalties for Violations

### DPPA Violations

**Civil Penalties:**
- Minimum $2,500 per violation
- Actual damages (whichever is greater)
- Punitive damages if willful
- Attorney's fees

**Criminal Penalties:**
- Fines up to $5,000
- Imprisonment up to 5 years (for knowing violations)

### TCPA Violations

- $500-$1,500 per call/text
- Class action lawsuit exposure
- FCC enforcement actions

### CAN-SPAM Violations

- Up to $51,744 per email
- Criminal prosecution for aggravated violations
